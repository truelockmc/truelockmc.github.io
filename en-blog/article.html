Why Nature is More Important for Us Than We (Want to) Think

Emil S. • 20. Mai 2025 • Käsdorfer

Have you ever paused to wonder what life would be like if the trees, rivers, and skies suddenly vanished? Or if Nature “decided” to turn itself against us, unleashing floods, fires, and pandemics beyond our control?

We humans tend to think that we “tamed” nature and are the most powerful animals on Earth, but that is simply not true.

The Catastrophe of a World Without Trees and Animals

We would have no resources anymore. We use plants to produce many things, like houses, paper, and even most of our clothes. We use renewable energy, like wind, water, and the sun – all of this is nature. Even fossil resources like oil or coal are produced over millions of years by nature. Plastic is also made out of earth oil or plant fiber.

The complete air balance system would collapse. Trees produce oxygen, which nearly every lifeform needs. They also consume carbon dioxide, which is toxic for humans and will make us suffocate in high concentration. It’s going to get really hot too because plants cool down our climate.

We are going to starve without enough nature. It’s already starting now: first the insects die. You may think this isn’t bad since insects are annoying, but this has severe consequences. Crops and fruits will stop growing as they need insects to pollinate them. Rivers will get polluted as smaller insects usually clean them. Small animals like hedgehogs and sparrows will go extinct because they don’t have enough food. Then bigger animals like falcons and foxes will disappear. This collapses the ecosystem, leading to invasive plants growing fast and normal plants dying. Farm animals will be affected too. They eat seeds, vegetables, fruits, or grass, but those wont be avaible anymore, as they are not pollinated and worms and other tiny animals that normally refresh the soil will die too.

We are going to have water problems. Like I already mentioned, without animals cleaning our water, it’s going to get polluted. Also, trees stabilize the ground with their roots. Without them, less water will be stored, and consequences after earthquakes will be worse.

When Nature Turns Against Us

Wildfires are a big danger for our lives. We already saw that in California, where thousands of people had to flee, and hundreds died. Pushed by climate change, giant wildfires are becoming more common. A wildfire blasts carbon dioxide, monoxide, and fine dust into the air, kills animals and plants, and destroys homes.

Sea level surges are going to destroy our basis of life. With climate change, sea levels are rising daily. A few centimeters might not sound like much, but many small islands or coastal countries already needed to make emergency plans because they fear losing their homeland to water.

New pandemics and illnesses could become a big problem. Shrinking habitats force wild animals near cities, increasing the risk of new pandemics like we saw with the Coronavirus in 2020. A newer, stronger virus could wipe out many humans, maybe like the Plague in medieval Europe.

The Deep Truth: Our Limited Power and Knowledge

Nature is unpredictable. Computers are not truly random, they simulate randomness. Nature is truly random, making it impossible for us to control it. We can try to predict things like the weather or use animals like snakes to predict earthquakes, but often it's too late.

We don’t really know our world. Many animals can see, hear, and feel things we can’t. 85% of our oceans are undiscovered. We know the moon’s surface better than our own seas. New species are discovered and destroyed every hour due to habitat destruction and climate change.

Extremes overwhelm our best defenses. Disasters like floods, hurricanes, volcanic eruptions, and wildfires cannot be controlled. Millions die, especially in developing countries like Belize, Venezuela, Iraq, or the US (hehehahahaw), which don’t have strong food or healthcare systems.

We are not the smart ones, really. No Matter how we put it, we are not the smartest animals, if you measure Intelligence by the Size of the Brain, some whales would be smarter than Elefants and they would be smarter than humans, which would make no sense. If you measure it by the Brain Size in Relation to the Body there would be Tiny apes, much smarter than Humans. Most Scientists believe the most accurate measuring of Intelligence is to see how much whorls and grey matter a brain has per cubic centimeter. According to that Theory the only Animal smarter then Humans would be the Orca, Orcas live in familys, make strategies to hunt together and even mourn their Death. Humans have the luck that we have practical Hands to craft resources and weapons, but if Orcas had a different Body they would likely be the ones to rule the World. And how smart can you really call a Species, destroying the planet they live one, without having an alternative place to live on and killing millions of innocent other animals?

Climate Change and Dangerous Feedback Loops

Melting permafrost leads to rising sea levels and releases methane, which makes climate change worse. It also might awaken ancient bacteria, causing new pandemics.

The Amazon "dieback" leads to bad air, polluted water, and fewer resources. It also destroys habitats.

Coral reef bleaching reduces fish populations, removing a vital food source for many people.

Cultural, Spiritual, and Psychological Connections

Nature is a source of art, mythology, and identity across cultures. Many of our legends and rituals come from nature.

Nature is good for mental health. Studies show that more greenery brings more happiness and a sense of freedom.

Nature controls our rhythm. Air quality, temperature, and food all influence our internal clock and how we live daily.

Conclusion

Nature is essential for our economy, our lives, and almost everything in our daily routine. We need to remember we are not almighty and only have one planet. We don’t get a second try. So maybe we should start respecting that a bit.
